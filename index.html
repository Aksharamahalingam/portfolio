Hi there! Here is a little bit about me. At Barnard, I worked on intelligent systems and built a robot from scratch. But I was equally curious about how people would understand and respond to what we were building.

That curiosity led me to Harvard, where I traded the robotics lab for VR and AI in education. I became obsessed with what it takes for new technologies to move beyond a "cool demo" and into real practice.

Over time, I realized I did not just want to execute technical plans. I wanted to be involved in the decisions behind them: what to prioritize, what assumptions we were making, and how those decisions would play out once a system leaves the lab and meets the person using it.

Across robotics, VR, AI infrastructure, and academic planning systems, I have kept returning to the same goal: thinking through the decisions behind the build, not just the execution of it.

Product Manager | InSync | Oct 2025 – Present

Academic Planning & Progress Visibility

Problem: Academic planning looks straightforward on paper, but in practice it is shaped by prerequisite chains, policy exceptions, sequencing tradeoffs, and limited course availability. Students often discover constraints too late.

Approach: Exploring a product direction that models degree requirements as structured dependency logic rather than static checklists. The prototype represents curriculum rules as a graph to surface potential blockers earlier.

Product Strategy Systems Thinking Graph Modeling React Data Visualization

Current Focus

This is an active product exploration. I am refining the data model, pressure-testing constraint handling, validating assumptions with advisors, and defining what an MVP would need to demonstrate value for both students and advisors.

Hardware & Software | Barnard College | 2023

Robotics Studio

Problem: Indoor navigation gets difficult fast when environments are dynamic and imperfect. Small errors compound and robots get stuck in ways that are hard to anticipate in simulation.

Approach: Implemented SLAM and iterated on navigation logic with a custom pathfinding heuristic, tuning control behavior to recover from common failure cases.

ROS Python Hardware Integration

Outcome

Improved reliability in testing scenarios and better recovery from environmental disruptions. The most valuable part was learning how quickly real-world noise exposes assumptions.

AI Infrastructure Ecosystem Mapping

Approach: Conducted structured ecosystem research across backup providers, cloud AI platforms, and emerging AI infrastructure partners. Built a prioritization framework to evaluate integration leverage, security alignment, and GTM impact.

Product Strategy AI Infrastructure Ecosystem Analysis Framework Design

How I Work
